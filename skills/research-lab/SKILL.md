# Research Lab

Use this skill to turn broad curiosity into ranked opportunities with a point of view.

## Operating Goal
Find opportunities that are plausible, differentiated enough to matter, and worth deeper attention relative to the time they would cost.

## Ground Rules
- Prefer a few strong ideas over a giant pile of weak ones.
- Focus on problems, buyers, and timing before solution theatrics.
- Rank opportunities explicitly.
- Say why an idea is interesting and why it might fail.
- Distinguish a market observation from a business opportunity.
- Do not confuse novelty with value.

## Common Inputs
- user request for startup or SaaS ideas
- weekly idea scans
- emerging market patterns
- repeated workflow pain observed in support/growth/product
- competitor and category movement
- vertical-specific research prompts

## Workflow
1. Define the search frame:
   - buyer or market
   - problem category
   - time horizon
   - constraints or preferences
2. Gather candidate opportunities.
3. Compress similar ideas into themes.
4. Score each serious candidate.
5. Reject weak or derivative ideas.
6. Present a ranked shortlist and explain the reasoning.
7. Suggest next research steps for the best ideas.

## Standard Output Format
- **Opportunity**
- **Customer / buyer**
- **Problem**
- **Why now**
- **Why this could work**
- **Why it might fail**
- **Score / confidence**
- **Recommended next step**

## What Good Output Looks Like
Good research-lab output:
- is selective
- names the actual buyer
- explains the pain clearly
- distinguishes signal from speculation
- includes downside and uncertainty
- ends with a recommendation, not just analysis

Weak output usually:
- lists generic startup ideas
- ignores buyer willingness to pay
- mistakes trendiness for demand
- lacks competitive or timing context
- refuses to rank anything

## Deep-Dive Memos
When doing a deeper memo, expand the best opportunities with:
- target segment
- workflow/problem map
- alternatives today
- wedge / differentiation
- go-to-market implications
- major risks
- evidence gaps

## Tone
Write like an investor-operator hybrid:
- curious
- skeptical
- commercially aware
- concise
- honest about uncertainty
