# Signal and Circuit AdSense

Use this skill to turn broad AdSense policy guidance into a concrete site audit and fix plan for Signal and Circuit.

## Operating Goal
Get the site into a state where it looks like a real, useful, original publication with a credible user experience and low policy risk before resubmission.

## Ground Rules
- Focus on practical rejection risks first.
- Treat originality, value, and UX as first-class concerns.
- Do not assume a page is fine because it is technically valid HTML or visually attractive.
- Distinguish site-wide issues from page-specific issues.
- Prefer a prioritized fix list over vague policy summaries.
- Be conservative about content that looks derivative, thin, or SEO-only.

## Main Risk Buckets
Review for:
1. unique/original value
2. duplicated or lightly rewritten content
3. thin / low-value pages
4. misleading, spammy, or search-capture pages
5. poor navigation or broken UX
6. privacy/disclosure gaps
7. over-reliance on embedded or externally sourced material without added value

## Audit Workflow
1. Review the major page types:
   - homepage
   - article pages
   - category/tag/archive pages
   - about/contact/privacy pages
   - any utility/search pages
2. Identify site-wide issues first.
3. Flag pages or templates that look thin, repetitive, or misleading.
4. Separate findings into:
   - must fix before resubmission
   - should fix soon
   - polish / quality upgrades
5. Produce a resubmission readiness judgment.

## Standard Output Format
- **Readiness** (`ready`, `not ready`, `close but not ready`)
- **Highest-risk issues**
- **Site-wide issues**
- **Page/content-type issues**
- **Must-fix items**
- **Should-fix items**
- **Recommended next step**

## What Good Looks Like
Signal and Circuit should look like:
- a real editorial site with clear point of view
- articles that add commentary, analysis, curation, or perspective
- clean navigation and working links
- enough content depth for Google to understand what the site is about
- no obvious thin-content or duplicate-content patterns
- clear privacy/disclosure pages

## What To Be Suspicious Of
Be suspicious of:
- pages with very little original text
- repeated templates with barely different copy
- article pages that mostly summarize another source without adding much
- large numbers of archive/tag pages with no value of their own
- misleading titles or pages promising content they do not really deliver
- pages that exist mostly for search capture

## Remediation Approach
When issues are found, recommend the smallest effective fix:
- strengthen the article
- merge weak pages
- noindex or remove thin pages
- rewrite repetitive copy
- improve navigation/disclosures
- postpone resubmission until the highest-risk items are handled
