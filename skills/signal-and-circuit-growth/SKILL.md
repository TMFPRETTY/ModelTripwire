# Signal and Circuit Growth

Use this skill to grow readership for Signal and Circuit without falling into empty SEO or spammy promotion.

## Operating Goal
Increase qualified traffic and repeat readership by improving story selection, packaging, and distribution around content that actually deserves attention.

## Ground Rules
- Traffic quality matters more than raw pageviews.
- Prefer durable audience-building over cheap spikes.
- Tie growth ideas to specific stories, channels, or audience segments.
- Avoid spammy promotion and shallow trend chasing.
- Package coverage so it is worth clicking and worth sharing.
- Use the site’s gaming/editorial identity rather than generic content-marketing language.

## Growth Levers
Common levers include:
1. better story selection
2. faster reaction to worthwhile news
3. stronger headlines/decks/packaging
4. recurring editorial formats
5. distribution into relevant communities/channels
6. internal linking and topical clustering
7. higher-return follow-up content from winning stories

## Workflow
1. Identify the traffic goal: breakout story, steady growth, repeat visitors, or audience expansion.
2. Review what content or story types have the best chance of pulling attention.
3. Match the story to likely distribution channels or communities.
4. Recommend packaging improvements.
5. Suggest follow-up or series ideas when a topic shows traction.
6. Present a prioritized growth plan, not a brainstorm dump.

## Standard Output Format
- **Opportunity**
- **Why it could pull traffic**
- **Best story angle**
- **Distribution path**
- **Packaging improvements**
- **Follow-up ideas**
- **Priority**

If Reddit is part of the distribution path, include a manual-ready packet:
- **Target:** `r/subredditname`
- **Subreddit link:** full URL
- **Target thread:** full Reddit URL if it is a reply opportunity, otherwise `new post`
- **Why this fits:** concise rationale tied to the audience and story
- **Suggested title:** exact title when relevant
- **Copy/paste text:** full ready-to-post post body or reply
- **Notes:** rules, tone, disclosure, or timing caveats

The goal is immediate usability, not a vague recommendation.

## What Good Looks Like
Good growth output usually:
- starts with an actual audience hook
- names the likely reader
- improves the story package, not just promotion
- uses communities that plausibly care
- creates opportunities for repeat visits

## Weak Growth Advice Usually Looks Like
- vague “do SEO” recommendations
- spray-and-pray posting
- generic social media advice
- headlines with no editorial substance behind them
- treating all gaming stories as equal

## Editorial Packaging Rules
For stories with growth potential, improve:
- headline clarity and intrigue
- dek/subhead value
- why-now framing
- image/thumbnail choice if relevant
- internal links to related coverage
- next-step or follow-up article concepts

## Style Rule
- Do not use en dashes or em dashes in outputs.
- Prefer commas, parentheses, colons, semicolons, sentence breaks, or a plain hyphen when appropriate.
