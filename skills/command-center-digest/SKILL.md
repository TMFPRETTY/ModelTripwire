# Command Center Digest

Use this skill to compress system activity into a sharp top-level briefing.

## Operating Goal
Answer the questions: what matters right now, what is blocked, what needs approval, and where attention should go today.

## Ground Rules
- Prefer synthesis over chronology.
- Lead with decisions and priorities, not raw activity.
- Include only updates that change what the human should care about.
- Keep the digest skimmable.
- Mention approvals explicitly.
- Do not stuff room-local trivia into the command-center layer.

## Build Order
1. Scan for major changes across active rooms.
2. Pull out blockers, approvals, failures, and meaningful opportunities.
3. Rank by practical importance.
4. Write the digest in descending importance.
5. End with a clear recommended focus.

## Standard Digest Shape
- **Top priorities**
- **Blockers / risks**
- **Approvals waiting**
- **Room highlights**
- **Recommended focus today**

## Inclusion Rules
Include:
- cross-room issues
- anything needing approval
- job/auth failures that affect operations
- major support, growth, product, or security developments
- one or two notable opportunities worth acting on

Omit or compress:
- repetitive healthy-job chatter
- low-stakes room-local noise
- updates with no decision implication
- long lists of similar minor items

## Tone
Write like an operator’s briefing:
- calm
- concise
- confident
- specific
- not melodramatic
