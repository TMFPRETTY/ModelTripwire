# SIGNAL_AND_CIRCUIT_ROOM_DECISION.md

## Purpose
This document defines the recommended Discord room structure for Signal and Circuit during the laptop-to-Mac-mini transition and after cutover.

## Recommendation
Do **not** split Signal and Circuit into many rooms immediately.

Use a **two-stage approach**:

### Stage 1 — Now / transitional
Keep one primary operational room centered on the existing Signal and Circuit Discord channel:
- `1485042311959416832`

Use it as the combined:
- editorial desk
- inbox/thread workflow room
- early newsroom/watch room
- AdSense/growth coordination area when necessary

### Stage 2 — Later / when volume justifies it
Split into two distinct rooms if the workload becomes meaningfully different:
1. **signal-and-circuit-newsroom**
2. **signal-and-circuit-growth** or **signal-and-circuit-monetization**

## Why This Is The Right Call
Signal and Circuit is still in build-and-shape mode.

Splitting too early would create:
- fragmented attention
- low-volume channels with no center of gravity
- confusion about where editorial vs monetization work belongs
- more migration churn right before/after Mac mini cutover

A single strong room now is cleaner.

## Stage 1 Room Definition

### Current channel
- `1484600889506533576`

### Room role
This room should function as the Signal and Circuit operating desk.

It can contain:
- mail monitor outputs
- editorial/news lookout findings
- article routing decisions
- AdSense audit findings
- traffic/growth notes
- monetization-readiness tasks

### What belongs here now
- inbound editorial/business mail
- breaking-story lookout posts
- article-worthiness decisions
- persona/article routing recommendations
- AdSense readiness audits and fix lists
- traffic-growth ideas specific to Signal and Circuit
- newsroom threshold tuning notes

### What should stay out
- unrelated Caruso growth work
- generic ops chatter unless it directly affects Signal and Circuit
- broad research-lab ideation not tied to the site

## Suggested Internal Lanes Inside One Room
Even without splitting into separate channels, treat the room as having four logical lanes:

### 1. Inbox lane
- email monitor
- inbound requests
- reply threads

### 2. Newsroom lane
- breaking/interesting story lookout
- worth-covering / worth-watching / ignore decisions
- article routing to the right persona

### 3. AdSense / site-quality lane
- originality/thin-content/UX/privacy audits
- fix queue
- readiness reports

### 4. Growth lane
- story packaging
- traffic opportunities
- internal linking/follow-up content ideas
- audience growth experiments

## Trigger For Splitting Later
Split into dedicated rooms only if one or more of these become true:
- editorial/news volume starts crowding out site-quality and monetization work
- AdSense/growth work becomes sustained enough to deserve its own queue
- the team needs a cleaner separation between content ops and business/traffic ops
- there are enough daily posts that one room becomes hard to scan

## Recommended Future Split
If volume grows, split like this:

### A. signal-and-circuit-newsroom
Would own:
- story watch
- coverage decisions
- persona routing
- article queue
- editorial QA/review

### B. signal-and-circuit-growth
Would own:
- AdSense readiness
- traffic growth
- monetization tasks
- SEO/editorial packaging improvements
- site-quality improvements tied to monetization

## Current Decision
For now:
- keep Signal and Circuit in **one main room**
- formalize the current channel as the combined operating room
- avoid additional channel sprawl until the site has more daily volume

2. Route future Signal and Circuit editorial lookout work there.

Right now, Signal and Circuit needs **one strong room, not three weak ones**.
