# SIGNAL_AND_CIRCUIT_ADSENSE_PLAN.md

## Purpose
This is the fast-action plan for getting Signal and Circuit into stronger shape for Google AdSense approval once the Mac mini is online and the relevant agents can work continuously.

## Goal
Move Signal and Circuit toward a state where it clearly looks like:
- a real editorial publication
- a site with original and valuable content
- a site with low duplicate/thin-content risk
- a site with reasonable UX and trust signals
- a site ready for a cleaner AdSense resubmission

## Working Assumption
The most likely reasons Signal and Circuit could struggle with AdSense are not the extreme policy categories, but the practical quality/compliance buckets:
- insufficient original value on some content
- duplicate or near-duplicate page patterns
- thin / low-value pages
- weak trust/utility pages
- weak navigation / archive / taxonomy UX
- search-spam-style patterns or pages that feel made for capture more than readers

## Core Agents To Use
- `signal-and-circuit-adsense` — audit and fix prioritization
- `signal-and-circuit-growth` — improve packaging, traffic quality, and repeat-reader logic
- `qa-review` — sanity-check major edits and policy-sensitive recommendations
- `ops-desk` — track fix progress and blockers

## Phase 1 — Audit The Site
Goal: understand the real site-wide and template-level risks before touching everything.

### Audit page types
Review at minimum:
- homepage
- article pages
- category pages
- tag pages
- archive pages
- author pages if they exist
- search pages if public
- About page
- Contact page
- Privacy Policy
- Terms / disclosures if present

### Questions to answer
- Does the homepage clearly communicate what the site is and why it matters?
- Do article pages add real editorial value beyond source recap?
- Are category/tag/archive pages actually useful, or just thin index pages?
- Are there repeated templates creating near-duplicate low-value pages?
- Are there broken, empty, misleading, or unfinished pages?
- Are the trust/legal/disclosure pages easy to find?

### Audit output
Produce:
- site readiness judgment
- top 5 highest-risk issues
- list of page types needing template-level fixes
- list of individual pages needing work
- must-fix vs should-fix split

## Phase 2 — Fix The Highest-Risk Problems First
Goal: eliminate the issues most likely to cause rejection.

### Must-fix buckets

#### 1. Thin / low-value pages
Fix by:
- expanding pages with real editorial substance
- consolidating weak pages
- removing or noindexing pages that should not be surfaced
- reducing empty archive/tag surfaces if needed

#### 2. Duplicate or repetitive content
Fix by:
- rewriting repetitive intros/outros/templates
- consolidating overlapping pages
- removing near-duplicate topic pages
- making article pages add more unique commentary/analysis

#### 3. Weak originality signals
Fix by:
- adding more commentary, curation, opinion, or analysis
- making sure source-based stories add original value
- avoiding “headline recap” style articles with minimal substance

#### 4. Weak UX / trust signals
Fix by:
- improving menu/navigation clarity
- ensuring pages load cleanly and read well
- fixing broken links / dead ends
- making About / Contact / Privacy easy to find

#### 5. Search-spam-style weaknesses
Fix by:
- removing pages that exist mainly for keyword capture
- avoiding misleading titles
- consolidating repetitive low-substance content
- ensuring page promises match page value

## Phase 3 — Strengthen The Site As A Real Publication
Goal: move beyond “not rejected” toward “obviously legitimate.”

### Editorial quality improvements
- strengthen article openings
- add stronger context and analysis
- improve internal linking between related stories
- make recurring coverage areas feel intentional
- reduce obvious template sameness across articles

### Trust improvements
- improve About page so it clearly explains the publication
- ensure Contact page is real and usable
- ensure Privacy Policy reflects actual ad/data behavior
- add or improve any needed disclosure language

### Site organization improvements
- tighten categories and tags
- reduce taxonomy clutter
- make discovery paths clearer for readers
- make homepage sections reflect real editorial priorities

## Phase 4 — Pre-Resubmission Review
Goal: avoid resubmitting too early.

### Before resubmission, verify:
- the site has enough original, useful content
- the weakest thin/duplicate areas have been improved or suppressed
- navigation feels coherent
- trust pages exist and are easy to find
- there are no obvious misleading, unfinished, or dead-end experiences
- the site feels reader-first, not search-capture-first

### Final output
Produce a final pre-resubmission memo with:
- **Ready / close but not ready / not ready**
- remaining risks
- recommended submission timing

## Parallel Track — Traffic Growth While Fixing AdSense
AdSense approval is better if the site also feels more like a healthy publication.

### Improve traffic quality by:
- better story selection
- stronger headline/dek packaging
- faster reaction to worthwhile stories
- more follow-up pieces on themes that hit
- better internal linking
- recurring editorial formats readers can return for

### Avoid growth tactics that make AdSense risk worse
Avoid:
- clickbait with weak page substance
- keyword-stuffed or mass-produced article patterns
- shallow trend-chasing
- low-value archive proliferation
- pages that exist just to catch search queries

## Fast-Action Checklist For Mac mini Arrival
1. bring up the Mac mini and core OpenClaw environment
2. restore/verify site-related workflows and access
3. run `signal-and-circuit-adsense` audit pass
4. classify issues into must-fix / should-fix
5. fix highest-risk templates/pages first
6. run `qa-review` on major remediations
7. run `signal-and-circuit-growth` pass for packaging and traffic improvements
8. do final readiness check
9. resubmit when the site materially feels stronger, not just technically cleaner

## Likely Deliverables To Create From This Plan
- page-type audit spreadsheet or markdown checklist
- top-risk issue list
- remediation queue
- trust-page improvement tasks
- content/template rewrite tasks
- final pre-resubmission memo

## Definition of Success
Success is not just “submitted to AdSense again.”
Success is:
- Signal and Circuit looks more original and editorially real
- low-value surfaces are reduced
- trust/UX is clearly better
- growth improvements support better readership
- the site is in a materially stronger position for AdSense approval
